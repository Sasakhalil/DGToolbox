What is EXIF Data?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) data serves as a digital fingerprint for your photos. It contains critical metadata such as Camera Model, ISO Speed, Aperture, and most importantly, GPS Coordinates (Latitude/Longitude).

Why Remove Metadata?

Removing hidden metadata is essential for privacy. Cyber-criminals, stalkers, or OSINT investigators can use embedded GPS tags to locate exactly where a photo was taken (e.g., your home address). SPECTRE allows you to sanitize this data instantly before sharing photos on social media or forums.

Features of SPECTRE:

  • Hex Dump Analysis: View the raw binary data of your image files.
  • GPS Mapping: Instantly plot embedded coordinates on Google Maps.
  • JSON Export: Download a full structured report of your file's metadata.
  • Privacy Sanitization: Strip all tracking data with one click (Client-Side).
